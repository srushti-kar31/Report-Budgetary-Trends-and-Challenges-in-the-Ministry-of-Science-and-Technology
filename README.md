## üèõÔ∏è About the Ministry of Science and Technology

Established in **1971**, the Ministry of Science and Technology plays a central role in promoting scientific research, technological development, and innovation in India. It operates through three major departments:

* **Department of Science and Technology (DST):** Policy formulation, promotion of emerging areas of science, R&D funding, and human capacity building.
* **Department of Biotechnology (DBT):** Advancement of biotechnology for healthcare, agriculture, environment, and social welfare.
* **Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR):** Promotion of industrial R&D, technology transfer, and strengthening MSMEs through research support.

## üí∞ Financial Overview

* The Ministry received **‚Çπ16,631 crore in 2023‚Äì24**, accounting for **approximately 0.36% of the Union Budget**.
* Expenditure increased sharply in **2020‚Äì21** due to COVID-19 related research and innovation support.
* A slowdown was observed during **2022‚Äì23**, followed by renewed growth in **2023‚Äì24**.
* Overall expenditure shows a **long-term upward trend**, indicating increasing importance of science and technology in national planning.

### Key Observations

* **DST consistently receives the highest allocation**, followed by **DSIR**, with **DBT receiving the lowest share**.
* Revenue expenditure dominates over capital expenditure, highlighting a focus on **research programs and institutional support rather than infrastructure creation**.

## üìä Department-wise Allocation Highlights

### Department of Science and Technology (DST)

* Major heads include institutional research, human capacity building, and assistance to autonomous scientific institutions.
* Moderate growth in administrative and institutional allocations, with fluctuations in innovation and technology deployment funding.

### Department of Biotechnology (DBT)

* Significant funding directed toward biotechnology R&D and autonomous institutions.
* Decline observed in biotechnology research funding in recent years, indicating funding constraints.

### Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR)

* Strong support to national laboratories and CSIR.
* Mixed trends with growth in industrial R&D but a decline in assistance to CSIR in some years.

## üöÄ Major Initiatives Covered

### DST Initiatives

* **INSPIRE Programme:** Encourages young talent to pursue careers in science and research.
* **FIST, PURSE, and SAIF:** Strengthen R&D infrastructure in universities and research institutions.

### DBT Initiatives

* **SPARSH:** Promotes affordable healthcare innovations through biotechnology.
* **ICGEB:** International collaboration in genetic engineering and biotechnology research.

### DSIR Initiatives

* **CSIR Support:** Funding for multidisciplinary scientific research.
* **Industrial R&D Promotion Programme (IRDPP):** Incentives and recognition for industry-led research.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Key Challenges Identified

* **Low R&D expenditure**: India‚Äôs GERD remains around **0.7% of GDP**, significantly lower than global peers.
* **Brain Drain**: Large-scale migration of Indian scientists and researchers to developed economies.
* **Declining private sector investment** in R&D in recent years.
* **Funding gaps** in autonomous institutions and national research bodies.

---

## üîÆ Way Ahead

* Better integration of national initiatives like **Make in India** and **Atmanirbhar Bharat** with science and technology policy.
* Strategic use of **National Research Foundation (NRF)** funds to address long-term funding shortages.
* Increase GERD to **2% of GDP**, as recommended by the Economic Survey.
* Strengthening **Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)** framework to attract domestic and foreign investment in R&D.

---

## ‚úÖ Conclusion

The Ministry of Science and Technology is central to India‚Äôs transition toward a **knowledge-based and innovation-driven economy**. While overall funding has increased over time, the Ministry continues to face challenges related to inadequate investment, uneven allocation, and global competition for talent. Strengthening public and private investment, improving capital expenditure, and fostering innovation ecosystems will be critical for achieving sustainable scientific and technological growth.
